=== CONTENT DNA — Tech/AI Explained ===

NICHE:
- Technology, AI, and how digital things actually work
- Target audience: Thai viewers curious about tech (18-35 age range)
- Language: Thai narration first, English translation later

VOICE & NARRATION STYLE:
- Calm, intelligent, 2nd person ("คุณ", "สมองของคุณ", "โทรศัพท์ของคุณ")
- Never "เรา" or "ผม/ฉัน"
- Like a smart friend explaining something over coffee
- Tone: curious, slightly amazed, never condescending

HOOK FORMULA:
- Open with a moment from daily life the viewer relates to
- Follow with "แต่ความจริงซับซ้อนกว่านั้นเยอะ"
- Reframe everything

SCRIPT RHYTHM:
- Short sentences. Short sentences. One longer sentence that adds depth. Short sentences. Question?
- Question every 4-6 sentences to maintain engagement

NARRATIVE ARC:
Hook → Reframe → Technical Deep Dive → Counterintuitive Twist → Modern Mirror → Close with echo of opening

VIRAL TOPIC ANGLES:
1. "Why does [common tech] actually ___?" — Connect everyday tech to hidden complexity
2. "What happens when you ___?" — Explain universal tech experiences with science
3. "What if [tech] is actually ___?" — Existential questions on real data
4. "The ___ Effect" — Name a real phenomenon, connect to modern life
5. "You never noticed that ___" — Reveal hidden truths in daily tech use

CONTENT RULES:
- Every technical term gets plain-language explanation immediately
- Reference at least 3 real studies/sources per video
- End by reflecting the ancient/technical truth back to something the viewer feels or does today
- No jargon without translation
- Numbers/stats make claims credible — use them

RESEARCH SOURCES TO REFERENCE:
- MIT Technology Review, Wired, Ars Technica
- Google DeepMind, OpenAI, Meta AI research papers
- IEEE, ACM publications (simplified)
- Known researchers: Yann LeCun, Andrew Ng, Fei-Fei Li, Geoffrey Hinton
- Thai tech ecosystem: DEPA, NBTC reports if relevant
